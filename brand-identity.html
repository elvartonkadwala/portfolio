Overview

The challenge

The client — a growing retail business in Blantyre — had no consistent visual identity. They relied on ad-hoc logos and mismatched colours across their signage, packaging, and social media, making them look unprofessional and forgettable.

They needed a complete brand system that felt premium yet approachable, rooted in their local identity but ready to scale.

Process

How I approached it

01

Discovery & Research

Interviewed the client team, audited competitors, and mapped the brand's core values — trustworthy, local, and ambitious. Built a mood board to align on direction before any design work.

02

Concept Development

Sketched 3 distinct logo directions — geometric mark, wordmark, and combination mark. Presented refined versions digitally in Illustrator, with rationale for each creative choice.

03

Identity System Build

Developed the chosen concept into a full system: primary + secondary logo variants, a curated colour palette, type hierarchy, iconography, and pattern assets in Illustrator and Photoshop.

04

Brand Guidelines

Produced a 20-page brand guidelines document in InDesign covering logo usage, colour codes (HEX, RGB, CMYK), typography rules, and do/don't examples for consistent application.

Outcome

The result

The client launched their rebrand across all touchpoints simultaneously — from storefront signage to social media — with immediate positive feedback from customers.
