Overview

The challenge

A local Malawian fashion brand had a strong product line but their social media looked inconsistent — different fonts, colours and layouts on every post. Followers couldn't immediately recognise their content in a busy feed.

They needed a complete set of on-brand, easy-to-use templates their team could update weekly without needing a designer each time.

Process

How I approached it

01

Audit & Strategy

Reviewed 3 months of past posts to identify what was working visually. Mapped out content categories — product shots, promotions, behind-the-scenes, testimonials — and created a posting strategy framework.

02

Style Direction

Defined a visual language: a curated colour palette pulled from the brand's existing colours, two complementary font pairings, and a set of graphic elements (lines, shapes, overlays) for consistency.

03

Template Design

Designed 40+ editable templates in Canva (shareable with the client's team) covering: feed posts in 3 formats, Instagram Stories (9×16), Facebook cover banners, and Reels cover frames.

04

Handover & Training

Delivered all templates with a simple usage guide so the client's team could swap images and text confidently. Included a content calendar template to keep posting consistent.

Outcome

The result

Within 60 days of launching the new templates the brand saw measurable growth in engagement and a significant jump in follower count — because their content now looked cohesive and professional every single day.
